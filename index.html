Anemone:

Their common name is windflower with the name "anemone" coming from the Greek for "daughter of the wind"
The petals close up at night and reopen in the morning to protect itself from cold nights.
anemones can often also represent relaxation.

Ranunculus:

Its name is derived from the Latin terms rana and unculus which translate to little frog.
Due to habitat destruction and the influence of invasive species, some Ranunculus species are considered endangered in the wild
Ranunculus symbolizes charm and attraction.

Lavender:

The word “lavender” comes from the Latin lavare (“to wash”) — the Romans used the fragrant oil in their baths.
Lavender flowers represent purity, silence, devotion, serenity, grace, and calmness.

Lace Flower:

AKA Wild Carrot or Birds nest
Represents Sanctuary

Cosmos:

The cosmos flower gets its name from the Greek word kosmos, which means “world”, “order” or “a harmonious arrangement”
Cosmos flowers represent order, harmony and balance, peace, tranquility, innocence, modesty and joy.
